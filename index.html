Crypto currencies


A cryptocurrency, also known as a crypto-currency, is a type of digital currency that operates over a computer network and is not dependent on a centralized authority, like a bank or government, to be maintained or upheld.



(Bitcoin)
The first decentralized cryptocurrency is called Bitcoin. Without centralized control, nodes in the peer-to-peer bitcoin network use encryption to validate transactions and log them in a publicly accessible distributed ledger known as a blockchain.



(Dogecoin)
Software programmers Billy Markus and Jackson Palmer decided to make a payment system as a joke, mocking the frenzied speculation in cryptocurrencies at the time, and launched Dogecoin. Both the "meme coin" and, more precisely, the "dog coin" are regarded as being the first.



(ETH)
Ethereum is a blockchain that is decentralized and has smart contract capabilities. The platform's native cryptocurrency is called Ether. In terms of market capitalization, ether is the second most valuable cryptocurrency behind bitcoin. The program is open-source. Programmer Vitalik Buterin came up with the idea for Ethereum in 2013.
